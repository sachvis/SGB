// HOW IT HELPS

Empowering Navigation
Through Technology

The Smart Glasses system uses a combination of ultrasonic sensing, audio feedback, and haptic vibration to guide visually impaired users safely.

📡

Real-time Detection

Ultrasonic sensors (HC-SR04) detect obstacles up to 4 meters away instantly, giving the wearer ample time to react and adjust their path.

🔊

Audio Alerts

A piezo buzzer delivers clear, distance-proportional audio feedback. Faster beeping signals closer obstacles — intuitive and immediate.

📳

Haptic Feedback

Vibration motors provide tactile alerts for users who may also have hearing impairments, ensuring multi-modal safety signals at all times.

⚡

Arduino-Powered

The Arduino Uno R3 microcontroller processes all sensor data in real time, controlling both audio and vibration outputs with low latency.

🔋

Rechargeable Power

A Li-ion rechargeable battery with a dedicated charging module ensures all-day operation with easy overnight recharging.

💰

Affordable Design

Total cost of just ₹2,300 per unit makes this assistive technology accessible to individuals, NGOs, hospitals, and rehabilitation centres.

Microcontroller

Arduino Uno R3 — the brain of the system, processing sensor inputs and triggering outputs in real time.

Obstacle Detection Range

HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor: 2 cm – 400 cm with ±3mm accuracy at under 15ms response time.

Power Supply

Rechargeable Li-ion cell with USB charging module. Estimated 6–8 hours active use per charge.

Output Modalities

Dual output: Piezo buzzer (audio) + vibration motors (haptic). Both activate on obstacle proximity.

Platform

Designed in Tinkercad. Open-source hardware — fully reproducible for research and education.
